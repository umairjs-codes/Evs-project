Tundra plants survive in one of the harshest climates on Earth. These plants grow in frozen soil, strong winds, and limited sunlight conditions.

Growth Characteristics

Most tundra plants grow low to the ground to avoid wind damage and retain heat. They also grow quickly during the short summer growing season.

Types of Tundra Plants

  • Mosses
  • Lichens
  • Dwarf shrubs
  • Grasses and sedges

Adaptations

Many tundra plants develop waxy leaves to prevent moisture loss. Some grow hair-like structures to trap heat and reduce wind exposure.
